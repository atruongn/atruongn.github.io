Disrupted the marketing for Lionsgate's Mockingjay Part 1 home release.

Lionsgate created a social activation to encourage fans to "unlock" a deleted scene from the film, with a faux progress bar to track the fans hunt. The website's HTML accidentally contained the deleted scene file link before completion.

The scene was leaked using a fake "official" Mockingjay Movie tumblr, created months prior using a comprehensive HTML theme that matched THG's official websites with automated posts from their official social accounts. The MockingjayMovie account garnered thousands of followers including prominent fansites who were led to believe it was real, and thought Lionsgate had posted the deleted scene too early before fans could actually "unlock" it.
